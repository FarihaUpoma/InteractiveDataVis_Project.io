Project 3: Calvin College 2004 Dataset

Fariha Moomtaheen

Visualization 1: Univarient Visualization(Bar Chart)

A bar chart showing the average SAT verbal and SAT math scores of the 271 students.

I have used color to visually represent the two types of scores for the SAT exam- SAT Verbal and SAT Math. The bars grow gradually when the page is loaded for the first time. I have added tooltip as animation to show the detailed information about both of the bars.

Visualization 2: Bivariate Visualization(Scatterplot)

The scatterplot shows the test score distribution of the students based on the student's other test scores.

There are two buttons to select the x and y axis of the scatterplot. If selected the dots and the x, and y axises will rearrange themselves to reflect the scattered relation among the scores. The mouse will select one dot when hovered over and the effect will be shown by the changes of color and shape of a dot. That selected dot will show data about that student's all the available scores.

Visualization 3: Easy Multivariate Visualizations( Stacked Bar Chart)

This is a stacked bar chart to show the average test scores of students who are grouped by their GPA score. I have grouped the students in 4 groups and we can see that the last group has the highest average scores. This represents that the students with higher GPA scores well in their SAT and ACT exams.

The bars will grow downwards when refreshed and the vibrant colors help easily differentiate among the different scores. If hovered over, the mouse point will show the data for that particular sector.

Visualization 4: Aggregation( Pie Chart)

This pie chart shows the percentage of students divided by their GPA scores. I have divided the students in 5 groups and demonstrated how many students fall in each group. We can see that the group with the highest GPA has the maximum number of students. This can represent the overall quality of the class.

The groups are divided by colors and the legend shows which color is used for which group. If we hover the mouse on any particular piece of the pie, we can see the details about that group of students. When the chart is loaded for the 1st time it will be created in a bouncy manner to entice the viewer.
